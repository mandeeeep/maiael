Case Study: SAGAR Distillery

SAGAR Distillery Limited
Q1 Loss of Rs15.32M ‚Üí +61% Price Surge

Fundamental Event: Reported Q1 net loss of Rs15.32 million on November 16, 2025

Market Reaction: Became the top weekly gainer with 61% price increase

Key Behavioral Factors:

Post-IPO Hype
Newly listed companies in Nepal typically rise for weeks regardless of fundamentals. Investors believe "ipo ko share mathi nai jancha" (IPO stocks only go up).
Supply Squeeze
Low selling pressure created artificial scarcity. Even small buy orders pushed prices rapidly upward as few investors were willing to sell.
Herding & FOMO
Early upward movement attracted more retail traders, amplifying momentum through social proof and fear of missing out.
Overreaction Rebound
Markets previously overreacted to poor expectations, causing a sharp rebound after the actual loss announcement.

Behavioral Insight: SAGAR's rally demonstrates how post-IPO psychology, supply constraints, and momentum chasing can override fundamental analysis in the short term.

Case Study: Nepal Telecom

Nepal Doorsanchar Company (NTC)
Rs13.2B Profit ‚Üí Flat/Negative Movement

Fundamental Event: Reported massive Q1 profit of Rs13.2 billion on November 17, 2025

Market Reaction: Price remained flat to mildly negative despite exceptional fundamentals

Key Behavioral Factors:

Priced-In Effect
Strong profits were already anticipated by investors, so the announcement didn't create new momentum or surprise buying.
Low-Beta Inertia
As a defensive, dividend-oriented stock, NTC attracts long-term value investors rather than short-term momentum traders.
Anchoring Bias
Investors remain psychologically fixed to NTC's typical price band around Rs800-900, limiting upside expectations.
Sector Rotation
Liquidity moved toward high-beta gainers like hydropower and microfinance, away from stable telecoms.

Behavioral Insight: NTC demonstrates how defensive stocks can underreact to positive news when market sentiment favors speculative, high-momentum plays over value investments.

Case Study: NRN Infrastructure

NRN Infrastructure & Development
42.105% Dividend Proposal ‚Üí Price Decline

Fundamental Event: Proposed attractive 42.105% total dividend on November 12, 2025

Market Reaction: Stock showed mildly downward trading pattern during the week

Key Behavioral Factors:

Buy the Rumor, Sell the News
High dividend was largely expected. When officially announced, investors who accumulated earlier began booking profits.
Psychological Resistance
Stock approached Rs2,000 level where many investors tend to sell due to anchoring and fear of reversal.
Bonus Share Dilution
Large bonus component implies temporary EPS dilution, motivating informed investors to exit before book closure.
Liquidity Migration
Market liquidity directed toward high-beta gainers like SAGAR and hydropower stocks, reducing interest in NRN.

Behavioral Insight: Even strong fundamental announcements can trigger selling when expectations are already high and profit-taking psychology dominates.

Case Study: SAHAS Urja

SAHAS Urja Limited
21% Bonus + Cash Dividend + 100% Rights ‚Üí Initial Rise, Then Fall

Fundamental Event: Announced 21% bonus shares, 1.1053% cash dividend, and 100% rights issue on November 11, 2025

Market Reaction: Rose on Nov 17-18, then fell on Nov 19-20. Still the week's top-traded company (Rs1.16B turnover)

Key Behavioral Factors:

Announcement Excitement
Initial buying to qualify for bonus shares and future rights eligibility drove early momentum.
Psychological Resistance
Price approached Rs700 level (touching Rs704), prompting widespread profit-taking behavior.
Dilution Fear
100% rights announcement signals future EPS dilution, causing informed investors to reduce positions early.
High Turnover = Rotation
Rs1.16B weekly turnover indicates heavy trading activity rather than long-term accumulation.

Behavioral Insight: SAHAS shows how announcement-driven rallies often lack sustainability when dilution expectations and sector rotation come into play.
