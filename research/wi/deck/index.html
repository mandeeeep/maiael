Introduction & Project Objectives

Project Overview

The World Bank Data Analytics Project integrates global development and financial indicators to support strategic, data-driven decision-making. Using data transformation, dimensional modeling, and interactive visualization within an analytics environment, the project consolidates multiple World Bank data sources to deliver multi-dimensional economic and financial insights.

Data Transformation Techniques
Implementing robust data transformation processes to convert raw World Bank datasets into analytical formats suitable for business intelligence applications.
Data Modeling & Relationship Building
Creating star schema architecture with proper dimensional modeling and establishing meaningful relationships between different data sources.
DAX Calculations
Developing advanced Data Analysis Expressions (DAX) measures to perform complex calculations and derive meaningful business metrics from macroeconomic data.
Interactive Visualizations
Building comprehensive dashboards with interactive features to facilitate strategic business decision-making using global development data.

Analytical Framework

Multi-Dimensional Analysis Approach

Dimensional Analysis Framework
Combines Indicators (what we're measuring), Countries (who we're analyzing), and Time (when we're measuring) to support multi-hierarchy analytics and multi-dimensional reporting.
Country Classification Framework
Countries categorized by income level: High Income (benchmark targets), Upper Middle Income (growth markets), Lower Middle Income (developing markets), Low Income (frontier markets).
Crisis Analysis Framework
Critical historical periods defined: 2008-2009 Global Financial Crisis, 2020-2022 COVID-19 Pandemic, 1990s Globalization Era, enabling resilience analysis.
Multi-Dimensional Workflow
Structured analytical process supporting exploration, comparative analysis, and risk assessment across multiple dimensions and timeframes.
